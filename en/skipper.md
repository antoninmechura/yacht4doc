# Skipper Guide — Internet and vessel data

---

## 1. Internet on board

The router connects to the internet via a mobile network. To use the internet for yourself and your crew, **insert your SIM card into slot SIM1** of the RUTX11 router.

> The router is located at _______________________ *(to be filled in by owner)*.

**How to connect:**
1. Open the SIM slot cover on the router
2. Insert your SIM card into the slot marked **SIM 1**
3. Wait approximately **2 minutes** — the router will automatically switch to your SIM
4. Connect to the WiFi network and internet will work through your SIM

**WiFi network:**
- Name (SSID): `RUT_SOUL_5G`
- Password: __________________ *(to be filled in by owner)*

> When you leave or arrive at port, simply remove your SIM — the router returns to the owner's backup network.

## 2. Vessel data — web dashboard

Once connected to the boat WiFi, open a browser and go to:

**`http://192.168.100.3`**

The dashboard auto-refreshes every 2 seconds and shows:

| Section | Data |
|---------|------|
| Navigation | Position, SOG, COG, heading, STW, log |
| Wind | Apparent wind (AWS/AWA), true wind (TWS/TWD) |
| Depth | Depth below keel |
| Engines | RPM, coolant temperature, oil pressure, fuel, battery voltage, engine hours |
| Environment | Sea temperature, air temperature, barometric pressure |
| Attitude | Heel (transverse tilt), trim (longitudinal tilt) |
| Batteries | Voltage, current, SoC (if available) |

> Data is only shown when available — an empty field or `---` means that instrument is not currently sending data.
